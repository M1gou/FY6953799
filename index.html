Introduction

The aviation industry faces a growing problem: climate change is happening and companies need to adapt. As more people fly, the number of planes in the sky increases, heightening the risk of accidents. By incorporating AI into this vast industry, companies can analyse massive amounts of data, detect patterns faster than humans, and support smarter decision-making. Beyond safety, AI also has the potential to reduce fuel consumption and emissions, saving airlines millions while contributing to sustainability. In this essay, I will discuss how AI is transforming our skies by making them greener and safer (Kabashkin, Mišņevs and Zervina, 2023; Demir, Moslem and Duleba, 2024).

The Current Landscape of Aviation Decision-Making

The aviation sector currently relies heavily on human decision-making, supported by automated systems. Pilots and air traffic controllers make rapid judgments based on complex data inputs, often under time pressure. However, traditional systems are limited by human attention and cognitive capacity. AI introduces adaptive learning models that continuously assess flight paths, weather patterns, and maintenance needs. Airlines are increasingly adopting predictive maintenance algorithms that can forecast engine or part failures before they happen, minimizing downtime and improving safety.
